Experience

Learning in the Real World

My sophomore year of high school, I had the opportunity to participate in a leadership program where we learned about nonprofit organizations. We learned the basics of what a nonprofit organization is as well as how it runs. During the year, we selected a local nonprofit and raised funds for one of their projects. Since then, I knew I wanted to work in the nonprofit sector or in a social enterprise.

I've spent the last three summers interning at Palomar: The Family Justice Center of Oklahoma City. Their mission is to help survivors of domestic violence find hope and healing. I've helped plan and carry out Camp Hope, their summer camp and mentoring program for children affected by domestic violence. I've also worked directly with the COO as an administrative assistant, ensuring that the day to day operations run smoothly. Some of my responsibilities have included filing Victim Protective Orders (VPOs) daily at the courthouse, creating schedules for Camp Hope, and crafting a newsletter to increase donor engagement.

During my sophomore year at BYU, I spent a semester completing an on-campus internship with Village Capital, an impact investing firm. Along with a team of four other students, I located, synthesized, and analyzed research on the impact investing ecosystem in Europe with a particular emphasis on sustainability and underrepresented founders.

I currently work as a teaching assistant for an advanced financial management class where I regularly hold review sessions, grade, and support the professor in teaching 120+ students. I also assist Dr. Flood, a professor in the French Department, with his research on polemical pamphlets from the Protestant Reformation. You can find some of my contributions on his website
